Who We Are

Hennops Revival is a nonprofit organization (NPO 246-266) focused on reviving, restoring and healing the Hennops River in Centurion, Gauteng. We collaborate with government, NGOs, NPOs, forums, the private sector, and the public.

Brief History

Founded by Tarryn Johnston in 2019 after severe flooding, Hennops Revival began as a community cleanup and grew into a broad movement for river restoration and active citizenship.

Mission

  • Government accountability through meaningful oversight and integrity of purpose
  • Corporate social investment and active citizenship
  • National government policy change; lobby via portfolio committees
  • National association of river cleaners; learning by doing
  • Influence representatives for realistic, sustainable implementation
  • Promote self-regulation that preempts legislative regulation

Vision

  • Collaboration and partnerships (municipal, provincial, global—public, private, people)
  • Job creation and expansion
  • Address water quality; educational drives and campaigns
  • From apathy to enthusiasm; proactive approaches; innovative solutions
  • Network of water custodians; empowered, uplifted communities
  • Clean, safe rivers and waterways; revitalized wetlands

Where We Work

The Hennops River flows from Kempton Park through Centurion into the Crocodile River near Hartbeespoort Dam. It is one of Gauteng’s most polluted rivers, requiring sustained community action.

Partners & Community

We work with municipalities, local businesses, schools, and volunteer groups to remove waste, restore wetlands, and educate communities about water stewardship.
